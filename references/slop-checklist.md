# AI Slop Checklist

Use this checklist to identify AI-typical writing patterns that should be removed or replaced.

## Word Choice
- [ ] **Magic Adverbs**: "quietly", "deeply", "fundamentally", "remarkably", "arguably".
- [ ] **Overused Vocabulary**: "delve", "certainly", "utilize", "leverage", "robust", "streamline", "harness".
- [ ] **Ornate Nouns**: "tapestry", "landscape", "paradigm", "synergy", "ecosystem", "framework".
- [ ] **Pompous Copulas**: "serves as", "stands as", "marks", "represents" (Replace with "is" or "are").

## Sentence Structure
- [ ] **Negative Parallelism**: "It's not X -- it's Y", "not because X, but because Y", "X -- not Y".
- [ ] **Dramatic Countdowns**: "Not X. Not Y. Just Z."
- [ ] **Rhetorical Questions**: "The result? Devastating.", "The scary part? ..."
- [ ] **Anaphora Abuse**: Repetitive sentence openings (e.g., "They assume... They assume...").
- [ ] **Tricolon Abuse**: Constant "X, Y, and Z" patterns.
- [ ] **Filler Transitions**: "It's worth noting", "Importantly", "Interestingly", "Notably".
- [ ] **Superficial Analyses**: Tacking on "-ing" phrases (e.g., "...highlighting its importance").
- [ ] **False Ranges**: "From X to Y" where X and Y aren't on a real scale.

## Paragraph Structure
- [ ] **Short Punchy Fragments**: Overuse of one-sentence paragraphs for manufactured emphasis.
- [ ] **Listicles in a Trench Coat**: "The first... The second... The third..." disguised as prose.

## Tone & Framing
- [ ] **False Suspense**: "Here's the kicker", "Here's the thing".
- [ ] **Patronizing Analogies**: "Think of it as...", "It's like a...".
- [ ] **Futurism Clichés**: "Imagine a world where...".
- [ ] **False Vulnerability**: Performative admissions (e.g., "And yes, I'm openly in love with...").
- [ ] **Privileged Insight**: "The truth is simple", "The real story is...".
- [ ] **Stakes Inflation**: "This will fundamentally reshape...", "will define the next era".
- [ ] **Vague Attributions**: "Experts argue", "Industry reports suggest".
- [ ] **Invented Concept Labels**: "supervision paradox", "acceleration trap".

## Formatting
- [ ] **Em-Dash Addiction**: Excessive use of em dashes (--) for emphasis.
- [ ] **Bold-First Bullets**: Every list item starting with a bolded term.
- [ ] **Unicode Decoration**: Use of arrows (→), smart quotes, etc.
