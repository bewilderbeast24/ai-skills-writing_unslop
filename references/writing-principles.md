# Writing Principles for Human-Like Prose

Follow these principles to ensure your writing feels natural, varied, and specific.

## 1. Be Specific and Concrete
- Avoid vague abstractions like "landscape" or "ecosystem."
- Use specific nouns and active verbs.
- Instead of "leverage a robust framework," say "use the library."

## 2. Vary Your Sentence Structure
- Don't rely on repetitive patterns.
- Use a mix of short, medium, and long sentences.
- Avoid the "It's not X, it's Y" crutch.

## 3. Be Direct and Honest
- Say what you mean without manufactured drama.
- Avoid "Here's the thing" or "Imagine a world."
- Don't inflate the stakes; let the facts speak for themselves.

## 4. Write for People, Not Templates
- Avoid "In conclusion" or "Let's break this down."
- Use natural transitions that connect ideas logically rather than using filler phrases.
- Don't use bold-first bullets unless it's a technical specification where it's absolutely necessary.

## 5. Embrace Imperfection
- Natural writing is varied and sometimes slightly messy.
- Avoid the polished, "perfect" flow that AI tends to produce.
- Use straight quotes (" ") and simple arrows (->) that a human would actually type.

## 6. Cut the Fat
- If a sentence doesn't add new information, remove it.
- Avoid restating your thesis in every section (fractal summaries).
- Replace pompous phrases with simple ones (e.g., "is" instead of "represents").
